# Federation Trust Overlays & Hub Meshes Architecture

Phase 80 introduces the interpretive and routing architecture on top of the federated registry and attestation hub network.

## Core Concepts

### 1. Trust Overlays
Trust overlays interpret raw signals from federations, converting metrics into a categorical `TrustOverlayBand` (e.g. `strong_bounded_signal`, `highly_fragile`).
- **Interpretive Only:** They do not authorize, they only contextualize.
- **Sovereignty Safety:** A high overlay score cannot override a sovereignty denial constraint. The denial manifests as a penalty capping the score to 0 and dropping the band to `highly_fragile`.

### 2. Hub Routing Meshes
Mesh routing models edges between exchange hubs, identifying optimal attestation exchange pathways.
- **Bounded Paths:** Routes generated by the mesh (`preferred_bounded_path`, `review_only_path`) remain strictly within the exchange bounds.
- **Degradation & Pressure:** Degraded hubs or edge connections under queue pressure get dynamically downgraded by Resilience Controllers.

### 3. Baseline Marketplace Signals
Signals from catalogs are ingested as `BaselineMarketplaceSignalRecord`. They supply weak, bounded, or useful hints.
- **Staleness Limits:** Stale signals are suppressed.

### 4. Ecosystem Resilience Controllers
Controllers oversee the ecosystem, triggering degraded states on critical mesh health or attestation gaps.
- **Health Guardians:** They only manage health limits, suppressing visibility but never authorizing execution.

## Future Extensions
This structure paves the way for Trust Overlay Exchanges, multi-hub routing scales, benchmark signal markets, and sovereign resilience fabrics in subsequent phases.
