# Closure, Deprecation, Maintenance, and Stewardship Guide

This guide covers the terminal lifecycle phase for the Sports Signal Bot ecosystem. Our goal is to close surfaces honestly, map deprecations explicitly, configure maintenance boundaries without hiding risks, and transfer stewardship ownership with full clarity.

## Closure Bundles
Closure bundles capture the state of terminal transitions. They must explicitly document blockers, caveats, missing visibility (no-safe or sovereignty), and residues.

## Deprecation Maps
Deprecation is not silent removal. It requires mapping to replacement workflows, keeping frozen states under review, or transitioning to archive-only access. It must preserve residue visibility and applicability.

## Maintenance Modes
Entering maintenance mode does not mean the component is risk-free; it means it is risk-bounded. We freeze features but we do not hide drift, legacy burdens, or rollback complexities.

## Stewardship Packs
Stewardship packs ensure that long-horizon components have assigned owners, defined escalation paths, and cadence expectations. We reject "unowned" or "ambiguous" stewardship gaps.
