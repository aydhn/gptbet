# Proof Freshness, Narrative Integrity, and No-Safe Visibility Guide

## Freshness Dominates
In the proof catalogs system, currentness is the primary indicator of evidence validity.
- A stale proof entry degrades narrative confidence.
- A stale observatory snapshot cannot be used to clear anomalies.

## Narrative Integrity
Narratives governed by Audit Boards must accurately reflect underlying proof states. Caveats and no-safe markers must never be stripped during summary or aggregation processes.

## No-Safe Visibility
If a system path or governance boundary lacks a known safe operational mode, this "no-safe" hint must propagate visibly through all proof queries, federated atlases, and narrative views. It cannot be overridden by abundance of proofs in an unrelated domain.
